01 — The Problem

"Most AI content tools are creative. This one is constrained — by design."

Large retail and beauty catalogues fail in two directions: copywriters invent claims the product can't support, or AI tools hallucinate ingredients and benefits. This pipeline solves both by separating what is factually true (the Product Truth Store) from what the market is saying (competitor intelligence) — and generating assets that are anchored strictly to the former, informed intelligently by the latter.

02 — The 7-Step Pipeline
Step 01
Product Input & Normalisation
Raw product data — ingredients, claims, usage, audience, brand constraints — is structured into a validated Product Truth Object.
Step 02
Product Description Generation
The Truth Object generates a headline, positioning line, benefit-led copy, ingredient highlights, and usage instructions.
Step 03
Competitor Marketing Intake
Structured competitor campaign records are ingested: headlines, hooks, CTAs, angles, tones, and proof styles.
Step 04
Pattern Analysis Engine
Identifies overused market phrases, dominant tones, saturated angles, and whitespace opportunities where the product can differentiate.
Step 05
Campaign Direction Generation
3–5 strategic directions are proposed, each with audience fit, differentiation rationale, proof points, and risk watchouts.
Step 06
Channel-Specific Asset Generation
PDP hero copy, social ad variants, email subject lines, category-card text, and promotional blurbs — format-constrained per channel.
Step 07 — Human-in-the-Loop
Review, Edit & Final Output
Every asset carries compliance metadata explaining why it fits the chosen direction and flagging any similarity risks. The user approves and deploys.
03 — Core Technical Pillars
⬛
Product Truth Store
The foundational factual boundary. All claim language, ingredient data, and audience constraints are locked here before any generation occurs. Zero hallucination is enforced structurally, not by prompting.
◈
Pattern Analysis Engine
Maps the competitive landscape by extracting dominant benefits, hooks, tones, and proof styles. Surfaces saturation — what everyone is saying — so the generated content can systematically stand apart.
◉
Risk Mitigation Layer
Three automated guardrails: Anti-Imitation (learns patterns without paraphrasing competitors), Claim Anchor (every asset must cite a Product Truth proof point), and Channel Constraints (format-correct per surface).

05 — Guardrails & Risk Mitigation
Anti-Imitation
Pattern Learning Without Copying
The system extracts messaging strategies from competitor data without reproducing specific phrasing. It learns what angles exist — not what words they used.
Claim Anchor
Every Asset Is Fact-Backed
Each generated asset must cite a specific proof point in the Product Truth Store. No claim can be made that isn't grounded in validated product data.
Channel Constraints
Format-Constrained Generation
Assets are generated to the exact format requirements of each channel: curiosity-driven subject lines vs. identity-setting PDP headers vs. social hooks — never interchangeable.
